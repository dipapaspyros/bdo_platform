App1: Wave Resource Assessment

This application allows you to assess wave conditions in a chosen location or alternatively to obtain a wave atlas of a chosen region. The service allows you to choose between several datasets including the numerical models most used worldwide, in-situ measurements and satellite data. Obtained results include multiple wave characteristics, which correspond to the first and very important step in order to develop wave energy related studies. The wave resource assessment (one location) allows you to have a first idea of the quality of the resource for energy applications. Multiple visualization options will allow checking, in a very straightforward way, how waves behave at the selected location in terms of: available power or energy; temporal resource availability (yearly, monthly, etc) and variability. The wave atlas allows you to visualize how waves behave in the selected area, displaying different important results such as average and maximum wave characteristics for the selected time frame.

App2: Wave Forecast

By using this service you will be able to quickly verify the wave forecast for the next seven days at one selected location. This is of utmost importance for different application such as: forecast wave energy generation; plan maintenance operations; avoid dangerous operating conditions, etc.

App3: Data Visualisation

This service allows you to quickly obtain a time domain visualization of any variable in the existing datasets in the BDO Platform. You just need to select a dataset and then a variable of the chosen dataset in order to obtain a time series graphical output of that variable. Using this service you can quickly verify data continuity problems such as those related to absence of measurements from buoys, or compare wave characteristics (e.g. wave significant height) from multiple sources. This allows you to quickly verify the accuracy of numerical models, when compared to in-situ measurements or to check how accurate satellite data can characterize wave conditions.
